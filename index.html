The SimuWeave Lab: A High-Fidelity Virtual Laboratory for the Principled Design of Wearable Computing Systems

Shi Zhuoyong*, Liu Xiaogang, and Other Authors
National University of Singapore
Nature XXX, 2025
Paper Code

We introduce SimuWeave Lab, a new simulation-driven paradigm for designing intelligent wearables. Our platform discovers superior, non-intuitive sensor layouts that enable clinical-grade health monitoring and power the next generation of multi-modal AI.

Abstract

The design of on-body computing systems, particularly those integrated into smart textiles, is often constrained by an empirical, trial-and-error process that fails to capture the nuanced, whole-body kinematic information essential for high-fidelity motor assessment. This methodological gap fundamentally limits the potential of wearables in sports science and clinical medicine. Here, we bridge this gap by introducing the SimuWeave Lab (SWLab), a high-fidelity virtual laboratory that establishes a new, simulation-driven paradigm for discovering sensor configurations based on the principle of maximal informational complementarity. By systematically exploring the design space in silico, SWLab uncovered a non-intuitive, asymmetric sensor layout that dramatically outperforms conventional designs in foundational tasks. Critically, we then demonstrate its true superiority in a multi-tiered validation framework of escalating difficulty. The SWLab-derived system successfully discriminates subtle, fatigue- and load-induced variations in gait invisible to standard methods and enables quantitative assessment of expert motor skills. Culminating our validation, the system achieves near-clinical-grade precision in quantifying pathological gait asymmetry, benchmarking against gold-standard motion capture. Furthermore, we show that this information-rich kinematic data acts as a catalyst in multi-modal AI, producing a synergistic, non-linear performance gain when fused with vocal biomarkers for Parkinson's disease assessment. By replacing empirical guesswork with principled, accelerated discovery, SWLab provides a universal instrument to engineer the next generation of intelligent systems capable of transforming remote health monitoring and personalizing digital medicine.
